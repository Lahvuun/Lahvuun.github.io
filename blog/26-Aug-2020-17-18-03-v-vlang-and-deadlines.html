V (vlang) and deadlines

Medvednikov's constant inability to keep up with deadlines (that he set himself) was amusing.
Maybe that's because I suffer from the same problem.
Knowing how Alexander handles criticism he would probably claim that I am jealous of his "success".
But the most likely reason is that I wanted to see his scam fail.

In any case, here's a few instances off the top of my head.

Open-source

Actually happened on Jun 22, 2019.

The 1.0 release

  • Jun 24, 2019: first promised for December 2019;

  • Jun 29, 2019: a notice about V being in alpha stage is added. Likely as a way to combat the complaints about the language missing pretty much every feature that was claimed to be ready: "Folks, chill, it's alpha, definitely not vaporware and I definitely won't run away with the money of dumb techbros who believe every fairy tale";

  • Dec 4, 2019: Alexander gives up on the idea of getting 1.0 out in December and renames it to stable 0.2 release. Makes you wonder what he spent half a year on.

The stable 0.2 release

  • Jan 1, 2020: 0.2 release date is moved to January 2020. This time the commit about this deadline shift is not even authored by Medvednikov;

  • Feb 7, 2020: release date is moved to February 2020;

  • Mar 2, 2020: to March 2020;

  • Apr 6, 2020: to April 2020. The commit is authored and commited by aj22r;

  • May 1, 2020: to May 2020. This one is authored and commited by logTom;

  • Jun 3, 2020: to June 2020. By scrungofan;

  • Jun 30, 2020: the notice about when the stable release is planned to happen is finally removed. It's replaced by a stability guarantee, whatever that means.

At the time of writing this (date returns Wed Aug 26 04:59:52 PM EEST 2020) 0.2 or 1.0 are nowhere to be seen. The latest tag is 0.1.29
